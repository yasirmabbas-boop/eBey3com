01 — Brand Strategy

The Digital بالة

ebey3 is the online mirror of Iraq's beloved bale and langa markets — the Friday markets, the Sunday markets, the treasure hunts where you dig through piles to find something original and extraordinary. We take that experience and make it organized, trustworthy, and always open.

The Problem We Solve

The bale market is a beloved experience trapped in chaos

Millions of Iraqis love the bale — the thrill of finding original brands, vintage pieces, and unique items at incredible prices. But the physical market is disorganized, inconsistent, and only available on certain days. There's no dedicated platform for this massive market. ebey3 changes that — we bring the bale online with proper categories, verified sellers, and the same thrill of discovery.

Mission

Organize the Bale

To be the first and only platform dedicated to the bale market — giving buyers the thrill of treasure hunting with the confidence of verified sellers and authentic items, all from their phone.

ننظّم البالة ونجيب تجربة سوق الجمعة لتلفونك
Vision

The Country's Bale Market

To become the definitive online destination for bale, langa, vintage, and pre-owned original goods — the platform people think of first when they want quality secondhand finds.

نصير سوق البالة الأول والوحيد أونلاين
Positioning Statement

For people who love the bale experience — the hunt for original brands, vintage finds, and unique items at great prices — ebey3 is the only online platform dedicated to the bale market, bringing the excitement and discovery of the Friday market to your phone, with organized categories, genuine sellers, and authentic items.

Brand Personality

Authentic — Real items, real sellers, real bale culture. No pretense.

Exciting — Every scroll is a dig through the bale. The next find could be incredible.

Organized — The chaos of the physical market, made clean and searchable.

Trustworthy — Genuine sellers, verified items, honest descriptions.

Who Uses ebey3

Treasure Hunters: People who love digging through the bale for original finds. They enjoy the hunt as much as the purchase.

Smart Shoppers: People who want original brands (Nike, Adidas, Casio, Levi's) at a fraction of retail — lightly used, excellent condition.

Bale Traders: Professional merchants who sort bales and sell the best pieces. ebey3 gives them a digital storefront.

Individual Sellers: People selling personal items they no longer need — their original, quality goods find a new home.

Language Strategy: Marketing vs. Descriptions

Marketing & Brand

  • أصلي (Original) — leads every conversation
  • فينتاج (Vintage) — for older, collectible items
  • ماركة (Branded) — emphasize the brand value
  • قطعة فريدة — unique, one-of-a-kind piece
  • بحالة ممتازة — excellent condition
  • بسعر حلو — at a great price

Item Descriptions

  • مستعمل (Used) — honest, transparent, no shame
  • مستعمل بحالة ممتازة — used, excellent condition
  • مستعمل بحالة جيدة — used, good condition
  • Clear condition descriptions with real photos
  • Honest wear-and-tear notes
  • Original brand name clearly stated

07 — Logo Usage

The ebey3 Mark

The ebey3 logo features a stylized Arabic shopping cart — a nod to the physical market experience. The icon mark (app icon) uses orange, the color of the bale. The wordmark pairs "ebey3" with "اي بيع" in Arabic.

Logo on Backgrounds

On Bale Orange (Primary) ebey3 اي بيع
On Navy ebey3 اي بيع
On White ebey3 اي بيع
On Cream ebey3 اي بيع

Clear Space

Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the "e" in "ebey3" around all sides of the logo. No other visual elements should enter this zone.

ebey3 min "e" height min "e" height

App Icon

The app icon uses the shopping cart mark on solid Bale Orange (#F15925) with 32px corner radius. This is the most recognizable element of the brand and must remain consistent across iOS, Android, and PWA.

Logo Rules

Do

  • Use the orange icon on navy or white backgrounds
  • Keep the Arabic name (اي بيع) alongside the Latin name
  • Maintain clear space and minimum size (24px)
  • Use the full wordmark in headers, the icon alone in compact spaces

Don't

  • Stretch, rotate, or distort the logo
  • Place on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
  • Add shadows, glows, or effects
  • Change the logo colors arbitrarily
08 — Brand Voice

How We Talk

ebey3 speaks like a trusted friend who knows the bale inside out. Natural Iraqi dialect, warm but not sloppy, knowledgeable but not condescending. We talk about the bale with pride — it's not cheap, it's smart.

Proud of the Bale

We never apologize for being a bale platform. The bale is quality — original brands, vintage treasures, smart shopping. We elevate it, not explain it away. "مستعمل" is honest; "أصلي" is the lead story.

Iraqi, Not فصحى

We use natural Iraqi Arabic — "لاگي" not "أجد", "حلو" not "جميل", "شلون" not "كيف". Our language sounds like the way people actually speak in the market. Kurdish content uses natural Sorani.

Exciting, Not Desperate

The bale is inherently exciting — we don't need fake urgency. "شوف شنو لگينا اليوم" (Look what we found today) beats "LAST CHANCE!! BUY NOW!!" every time. Let the items create the excitement.

Honest, Always

Items are used — we say so clearly. Condition is described accurately. Photos are real. Sellers are verified. Trust is built through transparency, not marketing tricks.

Tone by Context

Marketing & Social Media

Exciting, discovery-focused, bale culture. Share finds, celebrate the hunt, show the thrill. "اليوم لگينا ساعة Casio فينتاج بالبالة — أصلية ١٠٠٪"

In-App & Transactions

Clear, reassuring, efficient. "تم الطلب — البائع راح يتواصل وياك" (Order confirmed — the seller will contact you). Keep it short and confident.

Seller Communication

Supportive, empowering. Help them present items well. "صور القطعة من كل الزوايا — المشتري يريد يشوف الحالة الحقيقية" (Photograph from all angles — buyers want to see the real condition).

Auction / Bidding

Dynamic and naturally urgent. "المزاد يخلص بعد ساعتين — القطعة هسه بـ ٢٥ ألف" (Auction ends in 2 hours — currently at 25K). Let the countdown do the work.

Do's & Don'ts

Do This

  • Use Iraqi dialect: "دوّر"، "لاگي"، "حلو"، "شلون"
  • Lead with "أصلي" and "فينتاج" in marketing
  • Say "مستعمل" honestly in item descriptions — no shame
  • Celebrate finds: "شوف شنو لگوا اليوم بالبالة"
  • Reference the Friday market experience naturally
  • Treat bale as quality, not budget shopping

Don't Do This

  • Use stiff فصحى that doesn't match the bale vibe
  • Call items "رخيص" (cheap) — say "بسعر حلو" instead
  • Create fake urgency: "آخر فرصة!!! اشتري هسه!!!"
  • Apologize for selling used items or position bale as "lesser"
  • Use English when an Iraqi Arabic term exists and feels natural
  • Oversell condition — if it's worn, say it's worn

Font Stack

/* Arabic / Iraqi dialect (default) */
font-family: 'Almarai', sans-serif;

/* Kurdish (Sorani) override */
[lang="ku"] { font-family: 'Noto Sans Arabic', 'Almarai', sans-serif; }

/* English override */
[lang="en"] { font-family: 'Inter', sans-serif; }

/* Logo wordmark */
.logo { font-family: 'Cairo', sans-serif; font-weight: 800; }

RTL Checklist

Always

  • Set dir="rtl" for Arabic and Kurdish content
  • Use logical CSS properties (margin-inline-start, not margin-left)
  • Mirror directional icons (arrows, chevrons, navigation)
  • Test all UI states in both RTL and LTR

Never

  • Hardcode left/right values for layout
  • Forget to flip directional icons in RTL
  • Mix RTL and LTR text without proper Unicode isolation
  • Assume Latin font metrics work for Arabic script
