Price, condition, trust

Key insight: Your current cards handle the SEE stage well (good images, clean layout), but are weak at EVALUATE (missing seller trust, no bid count) and DECIDE (no countdown on cards, limited scarcity signals). The ACT stage relies entirely on navigating to the product page — there's no way to act directly from the card.
🔍 Detailed Issues
Ordered by impact on conversion
Critical

1. No Seller Identity on Cards

Cards show zero seller information — no name, no avatar, no rating.
Add a compact seller row: avatar initial + name + star rating + verified badge. eBay always shows seller name and feedback score.
Why it matters: In a multi-seller marketplace, trust is the #1 purchase barrier. Users in Iraq/Kurdistan need to know WHO they're buying from before they'll even tap the card. Without seller info, every listing feels anonymous and risky.
Critical

2. No Auction Countdown on Cards

Auction cards only show a static "مزاد" badge. The countdown exists in the component library (AuctionCountdown) but isn't used on any card surface — only on the detail page and swipe reel.
Show a live countdown (or "ends in Xh") on every auction card. Use red for under 1 hour, amber for under 24h, muted for longer. This is eBay's most powerful conversion lever.
Why it matters: Auctions without visible time pressure have no urgency. Users have no reason to tap NOW vs. later (and forget). The AuctionCountdown component already supports a simple mode — it just needs to be wired into the card.
Critical

3. No Bid Count on Auction Cards

The totalBids field exists in the schema but is never displayed on any card variant.
Show "X مزايدة" (X bids) below the current bid price. eBay shows bid count on every auction card — it's social proof that creates FOMO.
Why it matters: A listing with "23 bids" tells the user: this item is desirable, you might miss out, and the price is validated by demand. Zero social proof = zero urgency to act.
High

4. Image Count Not Visible

No indication of how many photos a listing has. Users can't gauge listing quality from the grid.
Add a subtle "1/6" or camera icon + count overlay at bottom-right of the image. eBay shows photo count to signal listing quality.
Listings with more photos convert better. Showing the count encourages taps and signals a thorough, trustworthy listing.
High

5. Condition Buried in Specs Text

Condition is concatenated into a tiny dot-separated specs line (9-10px) mixed with size, color, and material. It has no visual distinction.
Elevate condition to a small tag/chip on the image (bottom-left overlay) like eBay's "New", "Pre-Owned", "Refurbished" tags. Keep remaining specs as a secondary line.
Condition is the #2 purchase factor (after price) in secondhand marketplaces. It needs to be scannable at a glance, not buried in a 9px text string.
High

6. Price Not Differentiated by Sale Type

Both fixed-price and auction listings show price the same way. No "current bid" vs "buy it now" labeling.
Add a small label: "السعر" (Price) for fixed, "المزايدة الحالية" (Current bid) for auctions, styled in muted text above the price. eBay always labels the price type.
Users scanning a mixed grid of fixed and auction listings need instant context for what the number means. "25,000 د.ع" means very different things as a fixed price vs a current bid.
High

7. Inconsistent Card Templates Across Surfaces

4+ different card implementations: ListingHitCard (search), ProductCard (home), SellerProductCard, and inline cards in favorites/recently-viewed. Each shows slightly different data.
Extract a single <ListingCard> component used everywhere with consistent props. Variants handled via a size prop ("compact" | "standard").
Inconsistency breaks user mental models. If a card shows shipping info on search but not on home, users can't build reliable scanning patterns. One component = one source of truth.
Medium

8. Location Not Shown

City exists in the schema but isn't displayed on cards. For a regional marketplace, location is critical.
Add city as a subtle line with a map pin icon, below the seller row. "📍 بغداد" or "📍 أربيل".
In Iraq's marketplace, pickup-based transactions and local trust are major factors. Users want to know if a seller is in their city before tapping through.
Medium

9. "Negotiable" / "Exchangeable" Flags Hidden

isNegotiable and isExchangeable exist in data but only surface deep in the product detail page.
Show a small "قابل للتفاوض" chip near the price when the listing is negotiable. This is a significant purchase motivator.
Seeing "negotiable" on a card lowers the mental barrier to tap — users know there's flexibility even if the listed price feels high.
Medium

10. Free Shipping Not Prominent Enough

Free shipping appears as 10px green text below the price — easily missed during fast scanning.
When shipping is free, show a small green "شحن مجاني" badge with a truck icon, matching eBay's prominent free shipping callout.
Free shipping is one of the highest-converting badges in e-commerce. It should be a visible green badge, not whisper-quiet text.

🚀 Implementation Roadmap
Phased approach — each phase delivers standalone value
Great news: All data needed for the redesign already exists in your schema. This is purely a frontend effort — no backend or migration work required.
Phase Changes Effort Impact
Phase 1: Unified Component Extract a single <ListingCard> component replacing all 4 variants. Same data, cleaner codebase. ~4 hours Foundation for all changes
Phase 2: Trust & Identity Add seller row (name, avatar, rating, verified badge) + city/location to the unified card. ~2 hours 🔥 Highest conversion impact
Phase 3: Auction Intelligence Wire AuctionCountdown (simple mode) into cards. Show totalBids. Add price-type label. ~2 hours 🔥 Highest urgency impact
Phase 4: Visual Polish Condition as image overlay tag. Image count badge. Prominent shipping badge. Negotiable chip. ~2 hours Completes the card
Architecture note: Phase 1 (unified component) should come first because it eliminates the need to make the same change in 4+ places for every subsequent phase. The props interface should accept the full Listing type plus an optional variant prop.
🏷 Key eBay Patterns to Adopt
Proven conversion patterns from the world's largest marketplace

🔢 Bid Count as Social Proof

eBay always shows "X bids" on auction cards. High bid counts create FOMO and validate the item's desirability. Your totalBids field is sitting unused.

⏰ Time-Left Urgency

eBay's countdown is the most powerful conversion tool on the card. Color-coded urgency (red when ending soon) drives immediate clicks. You already have the AuctionCountdown component — just wire it in.

👤 Seller Feedback Score

eBay shows seller name + feedback percentage on every card. In a regional marketplace like eBey3, this trust signal is even more critical than on eBay.

🏷 Condition as a Top-Level Label

eBay makes "New", "Pre-Owned", "Refurbished" highly visible as colored tags — not buried in metadata. This is the first thing secondhand buyers scan for.

🚚 Free Shipping Badge

eBay's "Free shipping" is bold, green, and prominent. Research shows it's one of the top 3 conversion drivers in e-commerce. Your current 10px text is too subtle.

📊 "X sold" / Watchers Count

eBay shows "X sold" and watcher counts for extra social proof. Your quantitySold and views fields could power similar badges for high-engagement listings.
